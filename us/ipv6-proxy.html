What Is an IPv6 Proxy?

An IPv6 proxy is a server that operates using the IPv6 protocol. It converts IPv4 traffic into IPv6 traffic, allowing devices using IPv4 to access servers on the IPv6 standard. This enables anyone, even those with the older IPv4 protocol, to interact with IPv6 standards.

Similar to other proxy servers, an IPv6 proxy conceals your real IP address and location, enhancing your online privacy and security. It acts as an intermediary between your device and the internet, intercepting and translating IPv4 traffic into IPv6 traffic, ensuring connectivity regardless of your protocol.

Configuring IPv6 Proxies in Windows

  • First, you need to click on the Start menu on your device and open the Settings app.
  • Navigate to Network & Internet.
  • After that, click on Proxy on the left sidebar.
  • Turn the switch to the "On" position under the "Manual proxy setup" section.
  • In the Address field, enter your IPv6 proxy address.
  • On the other hand, In the Port section, you should enter the port number provided by your proxy service.
  • Click Save to apply the changes.
  • Finally, open your web browser and check your IP address to ensure it reflects the IPv6 proxy.

    • Configuring IPv6 Proxies in macOS

  • The first step is clicking the Apple menu and selecting System Preferences.
  • Click on Network.
  • The next step is choosing the network connection you are using (e.g., Wi-Fi or Ethernet) from the left sidebar.
  • Click the Advanced button in the lower-right corner.
  • Navigate to the Proxies tab.
  • You should now check the box next to the proxy protocol you want to configure (e.g., Web Proxy (HTTP), Secure Web Proxy (HTTPS)).
  • Enter your IPv6 proxy address in the Address field.
  • On the other hand, also type the port number in the Port field.
  • If your proxy requires a username and password, enter these details in the provided fields.
  • Click “OK” and then Apply to save the changes.
  • Open your web browser and check your IP address to confirm that it is using the IPv6 proxy.
